Introduction

1. Based on Imaging Techniques

  • Tomographic Imaging
    • Utilizes terahertz radiation to penetrate the sample
    • Acquires projection data from different angles
    • Reconstructs the three-dimensional structure of the sample through computational methods
  • Ghost Imaging
    • Separates illumination and detection paths
    • Modulates terahertz waves using a spatial modulator
    • Reconstructs the image through computational methods
  • Holographic Imaging
    • Utilizes the phase information of terahertz waves
    • Reconstructs the three-dimensional shape and internal structure of the sample through computational methods
  • Near-field Imaging
    • Utilizes the near-field effects of terahertz waves
    • Enhances imaging resolution through computational methods

2. Based on Computational Methods

  • Physics-guided Methods
    • Utilizes physical models and prior knowledge
    • Reconstructs images through optimization algorithms
  • Image Reconstruction
    • Recovering clear and complete images from incomplete or damaged data.
  • Super-Resolution Optimization (超分辨率优化)
    • Enhancing the resolution and details of the image.
  • Feature Extraction
    • Extracting useful information and features from the image.
  • Image Fusion
    • Combining terahertz images from different modalities or perspectives to provide more comprehensive and accurate information.
  • 3D Reconstruction
    • Reconstructing three-dimensional structures from two-dimensional terahertz images.
  • Noise Reduction and Artifact Removal
    • Improving image quality by removing noise and artifacts caused by instruments or the environment.
  • Real-Time Processing and Analysis
    • Developing algorithms and techniques for real-time or near-real-time image processing and analysis.
  • Hardware Optimization
    • Researching and developing hardware more suited for computational terahertz imaging applications, such as detectors, light sources, and computing platforms.
  • Diffractive Deep Neural Networks

3. Application Fields

  • Biomedical Imaging
    • Utilizes the ability of terahertz waves to penetrate human tissues
    • Applied in cancer detection, tissue imaging, etc.
  • Material Inspection
    • Utilizes the penetration or reflection characteristics of terahertz waves
    • Used for detecting internal structures and defects in materials
  • Security Inspection
    • Utilizes the ability of terahertz waves to penetrate clothing and packaging
    • Used for security checks and detecting concealed items
  • Art and Cultural Heritage Protection
    • Utilizes terahertz waves for non-destructive testing and analysis
